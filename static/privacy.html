This Privacy Policy explains how Pearnly ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses, stores, and protects personal data when you use our service. We act as Data Controller for account information, and as Data Processor for the invoice and accounting content you upload.

1. Information We Collect

Category Examples Source
Account data Email, password (hashed), display name, company name, country, phone You provide
Uploaded content Invoice files (PDF, image), bank statements, extracted fields You upload
Usage data Pages visited, feature interactions, error logs, device fingerprint Auto-collected
Communications Support tickets, feedback, emails to hello@pearnly.com You provide

2. How We Use Your Information

  • To operate the recognition, archiving, and automation features you request.
  • To authenticate you, secure your account, and prevent abuse.
  • To communicate with you about your account, updates, and support.
  • To improve product quality through aggregated, anonymized analytics.
  • To comply with legal obligations (tax, anti-fraud, lawful requests).

We do not use your uploaded invoices or accounting data to train any AI model.

3. Data Retention

  • Account data: kept while your account is active, plus up to 90 days after deletion for backups and legal compliance.
  • Uploaded original files (PDFs, images): tax documents and related source files (invoices, receipts, and similar records) are retained as required by Thai tax law (generally 5 years) and any applicable engagement agreement with our firm. Non-tax temporary files are cleared as needed. Extracted structured fields persist with your account.
  • System logs: retained on a rolling basis for security and debugging, subject to server storage capacity rather than a fixed number of days.
  • Billing records: kept for the period required by Thai tax law (typically 5 years).

4. Data Sharing

We do not sell your personal data. We share data only with:

  • Subprocessors strictly necessary for service operation (cloud hosting, email delivery, payment processing). Each subprocessor is bound by confidentiality and data-protection terms equivalent to ours.
  • Integration partners only when you explicitly enable an integration (e.g., when you connect your ERP or LINE account, the relevant data flows to that destination at your direction).
  • Legal authorities when required by valid Thai legal process or to protect rights, safety, or property.

5. Cookies & Tracking

We use a minimal set of first-party cookies and localStorage entries strictly necessary for authentication, session continuity, and language preference. We do not use third-party advertising trackers. You may clear cookies through your browser; doing so will sign you out.

6. Your Rights Under PDPA

Under the Thailand Personal Data Protection Act, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectify inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Erase your data ("right to be forgotten") subject to legal retention requirements.
  • Restrict or object to certain processing.
  • Data portability — request a copy of your data; we will provide it in a common, machine-readable format upon request. (Self-service export is not yet available; contact us using the details below.)
  • Withdraw consent at any time, where processing is based on consent.
  • Lodge a complaint with the Office of the Personal Data Protection Committee (PDPC) of Thailand.

To exercise any right, email hello@pearnly.com. We respond within 30 days.

7. Security

We apply industry-standard safeguards, including TLS encryption in transit, strict access controls (role-based access with per-tenant data isolation), hashed passwords, and audit logging. No system is 100% secure; if a breach affects your data, we will notify you and the PDPC within the timelines required by law.

8. International Data Transfers

Pearnly's primary infrastructure is hosted in the Asia-Pacific region. Where data crosses borders, we apply appropriate safeguards (Standard Contractual Clauses or equivalent) consistent with PDPA Section 28.

9. Children

The Service is not directed to children under 18. We do not knowingly collect personal data from minors. If you believe a minor has submitted data, contact us for removal.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated via the Service or email at least 14 days before taking effect. The "Last updated" date at the top reflects the current version.
